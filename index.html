First Post

Posted on some date in some year 2018

Skill or competency? This is a great question. How are we to know the difference? The biggest issue is defining these terms in a way that does not require physical proof. What do I mean? I mean that there is some level of mathematical justification that can be used to acknowldge the apriory of the definition written down. The only way to do this is mathematically, to satisfy a human's need for specification. Need, or want, further clarification? Consider someone explaining you a mathematical concept. And then you simply say, "I do not believe you." What is the person explaining the concept to do(bear with me I will connect back to my point, I will need to illustrate this)? They could simply state that the listener, student, does not possess the ability to understand. But then the student may claim that the teacher does not posess the understanding to explain. This issue be exacerbated until there is some precise definition presented, either by the teacher, or student. This precise definition may be manifested(proven) in many ways. When considering this manifestation of "proof" we might want to consider what is neccesary to present it: What skill? What competance? While I will not be providing resolution to this I am trying to point towards the issue at hand. I want to go on the record of stating that skills and competancy have nothing to do with attitude or person disposition. This is the notion of judgement coming in and there is a slipper slope towrds of less palatable aspects of the profession of educator: racism, stereotypes, etc. To avoid the appearance of such things we need precise definitions, mathematical in nature, of skills and competencies. Otherwise you will get people running rampant with the ability to "muddy the water" of the discussion to "cover their misconceptions".
